The core model

Every day resolves to one clear decision.

FitAI reduces noisy signals into a single recovery-aware verdict — then translates it into how hard to study, how hard to train, and how to recover.

Push Day

Spend the surplus.

High readiness, low residual stress, and good sleep. Capacity is available — this is the day to attack hard goals.

StudyDeep work
WorkoutHigh intensity
RecoveryProtect sleep tonight
Maintain Day

Hold the line.

Balanced signals with some load. Make steady progress without digging into reserves you'll need later this week.

StudyFocused blocks
WorkoutModerate / Zone 2
RecoveryLight mobility + walk
Recover Day

Refill the tank.

Low readiness, high stress, or sleep debt. Pulling harder now costs more than it returns — recover deliberately, then reschedule.

StudyLight review only
WorkoutRest or gentle stretch
RecoveryReschedule peak tasks

Primary Ghost

Glass panels & cards

Frosted blur, hairline borders, and large 16–30px radii. Layered glow behind, never harsh shadows.

Motion & microinteractions

  • → Cards lift 4–6px on hover with a soft border brighten.
  • → Sections fade up gently on scroll into view.
  • → Rings and bars animate in to their value, never looping distractingly.
  • → Transitions stay 200–300ms with calm easing — nothing bouncy.
Interaction philosophy

How FitAI should feel.

The product's personality matters as much as its logic. FitAI guides — it never lectures, alarms, or pretends to be a doctor.

◐

Calm but motivating

Encourages momentum without pressure or guilt.

◎

Decision-first, not data-first

Leads with the call; the numbers support, not bury, it.

◇

Clear, not overconfident

Speaks in confident guidance, but never false certainty.

✚

Helpful, not medical

Frames everything as lifestyle and productivity guidance.

◆

Premium, not complicated

Depth on demand; the default view stays effortless.

♁

Personal, not overwhelming

Adapts to you without flooding you with signals.

ⓘ
Not a medical product. FitAI is designed to offer lifestyle and productivity guidance only. It does not diagnose, treat, or make medical claims, and its recommendations are framed as suggestions to help you plan your day — not clinical advice.
